Documentation

Course Activity Planning can use dates from meetings to plan activities.

Meetings dates can not be changed as they are fixed by the school.

How to plan an activity relative to meetings

  • Find the key of the activity to plan. The list of activities is on the right on the text editor.
  • Find the meetings that will indicate the start and end of the activity. The list of meetings is on the right on the text editor.
  • Write in the text editor the keys as "key of the activity" "key of the first meeting" "key of the second meeting".

The first meeting key will indicate the start date of the activity and the second, its end date.

Repeat for each activity. Each line of the planning will modify the start and end dates of an activity.

Exemple

1. Quiz 1 (Q1) is to be planned

2. Quiz will open at Seminar 1 (S1) and close at Seminar 2 (S2)

3. The final line is written in the editor as "Q1 S1 S2"

Referencing to the start or end of a meeting

Planning relative to the end of the meeting can be done by adding to suffix "F" to the meeting key.

The default behavior is to use the start of the meeting if "F" is not present. Accesing the start date can be explicit with adding the suffix "S".

Exemple

Given Seminar 1 starting at 8:00 and ending at 10:00.

Quiz 1 will open at 8:00 and close at 10:00 with the following line: "Q1 S1 S1F" (or "Q1 S1S S1F")

Relative time deltas

A time delta is composed of + or - sign, an integer and one of the followings element

  • m (minutes)
  • h (hours)
  • d (days)
  • w (weeks)

This modified identifier can now be substituted in a planning line.

Exemple

One day before Seminar 2 is "S2-1D".

Quiz 1 will open at the end of Seminar 1 and close one day before Seminar 2: "Q1 S1F S2-1D"

Absolute time modifier

Append the "@" character followed by the exact time (hh:mm) to a meeting key: "key"@hh:mm

This modified identifier can now be substituted in a planning line.

Exemple

The day of Seminar 2 at exactly 23:59 can be written as "S2@23:59".

Quiz 1 will open at the end of Seminar 1 and close at exactly 23:59 on the day of Seminar 2 with the following line: "Q1 S1F S2@23:59"

Combining time modifier and relative modifier

Combining a time modifier and delta is possible. Please note that combining two deltas is not supported.

"key""delta""exact time"

Exemple

The day before Seminar 2 at exactly 23:59: "S2-1D@23:59"

Quiz 1 will open at the end of Seminar 1 and close the day before Seminar 2 at exactly 23:59: "Q1 S1F S2-1D23:59"
